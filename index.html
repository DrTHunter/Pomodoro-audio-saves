About Pomodoro Technique

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s.

How to Use This Timer:

  1. Choose a task to focus on
  2. Start the 25-minute timer (work session)
  3. Work focused until the timer rings
  4. Take a 5-minute break
  5. After 4 work sessions, take a 30-minute break
  6. Change tasks after each Pomodoro to maintain productivity

Key Productivity Tips:

  • Switch tasks every 25 minutes to maintain fresh focus
  • Use work notes to track different tasks
  • During breaks, completely disengage from work
  • Adjust timer lengths based on task complexity
  • Use the 5-minute breaks to stretch and refresh

Why Track Productivity?

Tracking your work time and accomplishments provides concrete data to demonstrate your value to your organization.

Benefits for Performance Reviews:

  • Quantify your contributions with exact hours worked and tasks completed
  • Show efficiency improvements over time with historical data
  • Document your focus - time tracking shows dedication to important tasks
  • Identify productivity patterns to optimize your work habits

How to Present for Raises:

  • Show consistent productivity with your tracked work hours
  • Highlight completed projects from your notes with time invested
  • Demonstrate work-life balance with proper break tracking
  • Show initiative by presenting your self-tracking system
  • Connect to business outcomes - link your time to measurable results
